# Business Intent — Inventory Management System

## Project Name

Inventory Management System

## Business Goal

Enable a small business to track product inventory accurately, respond to low-stock situations promptly, and generate simple operational reports.

## Problem to Solve

The business currently tracks inventory using spreadsheets, leading to stock discrepancies, missed reorder points, and no audit trail for stock movements.

## Target Users

- Business owner (oversight, reporting, alerts)
- Warehouse staff (daily stock operations)

## Expected Outcome

A software system that tracks products, stock quantities, movements, alerts, and reports — accessible to non-technical users.

## Constraints

- Must work for a team of 10 or fewer users
- Must be operable by warehouse staff without technical training
- Budget-conscious MVP approach

## Budget Considerations

MVP budget suitable for a small business SaaS or self-hosted solution.

## Timeline Considerations

MVP within one quarter; reporting enhancements in subsequent release.

## Data Sensitivity

Product and supplier data is business-confidential but not regulated personal data.

## Compliance Concerns

None identified for MVP. Review if customer data is added in future releases.

## Human Decision Approver

Business Owner

## Initial Success Criteria

- 100% of products tracked in the system within 30 days of launch
- Low-stock alerts reduce stockouts by 50%
- Warehouse staff can record a stock movement in under 30 seconds
